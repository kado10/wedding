Lucy Line

Oliviapool Street
images/oliviapool.png
Olivia Robson
The trains always run on time at this station, according to a strict itinerary. In fact the trains can leave early, but if you miss a train because it left early, well that’s your fault for being late. Remember that the manager is always right, and the customer is usually wrong. However as long as you’re adhering to the itinerary of the station, you’re sure to have a pleasant journey.
London Mansbridge
images/london_mansbridge.png
Henry Mansbridge
This station boasts the most up to date security system, although there can sometimes be issues, with fire alarms going off without warning. This station does not cope well with crowded rush hours, and enjoys a slower pace of life, with specified “relaxing times” scheduled in the timetable.
Bestminster
images/bestminster.png
Dani Best
The trains at this station are always late, if they show up at all. Some say a train came on time once, but most think this is a myth. Services run late into the night, and passengers seem to have a tendency to loose things, with the lost property filled with such items as debit cards, phones and pants.
Charing Josh
images/charing_josh.png
Joshua Reynolds
Tourists may struggle at this station, as staff only speak in pop culture references rather than plain English.

Katie Line

Lyns Cross St Pancras
images/lyns_cross.png
Lynsey Dowden
This station is famous for its fruit and veg stalls, you’ll find plenty of grocers in competition with one another over who has the biggest, longest or most misshapen banana. This is the part of the line with the most accidents, with passengers injured doing such dangerous things like standing up, or falling over when sober.
Wembley Stadam
images/wembley_stadam.png
Adam Dowden
Trains from this station head north… or south. It can be difficult to tell as all announcements are made with sarcastic undertones. Drivers here insist they are the best drivers on the line, but reviews from passengers use words such as “terrifying” and “scarring.” At least the electrics never break down though.
Karada Water
images/karada_water.png
Kara Mills
You can alight here for lots of vegan food stands, though late on a Saturday night cheesy chips also become available. This station is well equipped for any eventuality, first aid kits more resemble Mary Poppin's suitcase, with staff providing customers with water bottles, scissors and toothpicks when they have required them.
Olliephant and Castle
images/olliephant_and_castle.png
Ollie Smith
You are likely to see plenty of busking at this station, the musicians are very talented, but only ever seem to play “A thousand miles” by Vanessa Carlton. Staff here are also available for “fun” suggestions of what to do in London, like go to Big Ben and listen to the chimes, super fun.
Waterlucia

Lucia Amabile
At this station there are plenty of small curly haired dogs available for cuddles and stress relief after a long commute. A night service is most definitely not available, trials led to train drivers ceasing to function on a lack of sleep, some even falling asleep standing up in their cabin.
Location

The Dickens Inn, Marble Quay, St Katharine's Way, St Katharine's & Wapping, London E1W 1UH

  • Please note that as the venue is on the docks there is no parking whatsoever. Do not bring your car!
  • Taxi options are:
    • If you are coming by taxi the closest drivable road is Mews Street by Mala Indian Restaurant. It is then around a 60m walk to the venue.
  • Nearby train stations include:
    • Tower Hill on the District and Circle underground lines. It is then an 8 minute walk to the venue.
    • Fenchurch Street Railway Station on the Mainline. It is then a 9 minute walk to the venue.
    • Tower Gateway on the DLR. It is then a 7 minute walk to the venue.
  • Buses that stop near the venue include:
    • The 100 stops at Stockholm Way, Nesham Street and The Highway which are all a 5 minute walk from the venue.
    • The RV1 stops at The Tower of London (Stop TH) which is a 6 minute walk to the venue
    • The 42 and 78 both stop at Tower Bridge City Hall which is a 9 minute walk to the venue
    • The 343 stops at City Hall which is an 11 minute walk to the venue
    • The 188 stops at Druid Street which is a 12 minute walk to the venue
  • Boat options are:
    • Nearest ferry stop is St Katherines Pier, served by Thames River Services and Circular Cruises. It is then a 3 minute walk to the venue.
